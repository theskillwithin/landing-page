Fragrance

Ayurveda Botanicals

In a well appointed spa that uses the ancient practices of eastern Indian medicine, known as Ayurveda, you will find the blend of healing massage oils intoxicating. Relax deeply as the warm oils sooth your body and mind. NOTES: Bergamot, basil, patchouli, ylang, geranium, sage, mint.

Camomile

This delicate flower is most well known for its ability to lure one to sleep after a cup of tea is brewed from its leaves. It’s fragrance is warm, herbaceous and comforting. NOTES: Camomile, herbs, mint.

Cypress

A trail weaves through dense mountain woods like a ribbon, carrying you to hidden alcoves where cypress sentinels thrive. They band together in varying sizes, their pollen drifting off on the breeze en masse. NOTES: Cypress, hemlock, clover, mint.

Espresso & Cocoa

Inspired by a quaint Venetian café, this fragrance invites you to walk in and be enveloped by the combined nuances of espresso, freshly baked pastry, roasted nuts and dark Italian chocolate. Richly indulgent and satisfying. NOTES: Espresso, pastry, dark chocolate, roasted nuts.

Ginger & Clove

As one of the oldest spices known in Asia and Europe, ginger has a long history for its medicinal and culinary benefits. Ginger’s spicy fragrance married with sweet cloves and sprinkled with black pepper creates this warm and soothing blend. NOTES: Ginger, clove, cream, black pepper.

Ginger & Clove

As one of the oldest spices known in Asia and Europe, ginger has a long history for its medicinal and culinary benefits. Ginger’s spicy fragrance married with sweet cloves and sprinkled with black pepper creates this warm and soothing blend. NOTES: Ginger, clove, cream, black pepper.

Lavender & Lemon Peel

Not many scenes are more picturesque than the lavender fields of Provence. Take yourself there with this blend of calming lavender oil infused with the bright notes found in a lemon peel giving you a sense of cleanliness and restoration. NOTES: Lavender, lemon peel.

Mint Leaf & Lime

Reminiscent of an ice cold mojito on a summer evening, this blend of refreshing lime juice infused with the cool notes of mint will invigorate and brighten the mood of any environment. NOTES: Lime zest, mint, sugar, cool water.

Persimmon

The Persimmon tree, a member of the ebony family, produces a truly unique berry. Used heavily throughout Asia to sweeten dishes or make dessert. The dark orange fruit strikes the taste buds with a twist due to its sweet nutty flavor followed by a touch of cinnamon and clove. NOTES: Persimmon, pear, cinnamon, nutmeg, clove.

Redcurrant

Wild currant bushes laced with fruit sprout up from rich soil throughout western Europe. In varieties of red, black and white, the fruit can be sweet, but at times also bitter. This fragrance captures both the tart and the sweet notes blended with lush green leaves. NOTES: Currant berries, lime, green vine.

Sandalwood

Breathe deeply and clear your mind as you draw in the buttery, sweet notes of sandalwood. Used for centuries in religious ceremonies, sandalwood has long been praised for its rich, sensual fragrance and its ability to bring one closer to nature. NOTES: Sandalwood, vanilla, cream, cedar and musk.

Tabac & Vanilla Bean

This fragrance is reminiscent of a luxurious den with its fire stoked and dark wood shelves full of literary treasures waiting to be brought down and enjoyed. Rich and plush tones of tobacco, vanilla, cedar and musk all at once will entice your senses. NOTES: Cured tobacco leaf, vanilla, cedar, musk.

Wild Spearmint Bush

Hiking along a mountain trail that winds through the forest, you may not even notice the hidden gem of the wild spearmint bush, until a subtle breeze lifts its scent to you. Reach down, pick a leaf and breathe in this mountain treasure NOTES: Wild spearmint, evergreen, soil.

Cedar Wood & Vanilla

Used in aroma therapy for centuries to soothe anxiety and help clear the mind, the strong earthy fragrance of cedar mixes with the mild and comforting scent of vanilla beans to produce an extremely pleasant and soothing aroma. Perfect for adding a smooth, relaxed feeling to any room. NOTES: Cedar, pine, vanilla, sandalwood.

Cucumber

Slice into a green cucumber still glazed from the morning dew and savor a burst of garden freshness. Crisp and clean, its simplicity at its finest. Cool, green and refreshing. NOTES: Cucumber, cool water.

Dragon Fruit

A succulent treat indigenous to Asia, the Dragon Fruit has been cultivated for centuries and has spread to several tropical locations around the world. A favorite of the local porters who throw a few in their packs and crack one open for a needed refreshment on a sweltering day. NOTES: Dragon fruit, lemon zest, tangerine, honeydew.

Fig & Ivy

A staple in middle eastern culture and cuisine, the fig plucked ripe from a tree is not sweet, but woody, even nutty in fragrance. Mingled with green ivy and cedar, you get a refined and mature fragrance. NOTES: Fresh fig, bark, cedar, musk, green ivy.

Green Tea

Originating in China, green tea has now swept across the globe to be used in a variety of ways. From herbal beverages to holistic massage, it’s mediums are vast. It’s fragrance is also intoxicating; herbaceous and clean. NOTES: Green tea, clover, anise.

Magnolia

Like true southern hospitality, the magnolia flower is at once bright and welcoming. The massive flower abundantly blooms in spring and you cannot help but notice its bouquet of fragrance wafting on the breeze. NOTES: Magnolia blossom, gardenia.

Orange Flower

The essence of spring is difficult to describe, but certainly felt by all. As cold days give way to the suns warming light, a tangible excitement can be felt. This splendid fragrance combines sweet orange the green leaves of the tree, evoking a scent that is both fresh and new. NOTES: Orange blossom,tangerine, clementine leaves, water lily.

Plumeria

Plumeria, commonly called Frangipani throughout the south pacific, is a beautifully delicate flower often used in making leis. It blooms throughout the day, but increases its fragrance at night in order to lure moths for pollination. It has a subtly sweet scent that is both pleasant and inviting. NOTES: Plumeria, orange blossom, gardenia, jasmine.

Rosemary & Mint

These herbs are used in holistic medicine, but also in the culinary arts much to a foodies delight. Rosemary needles and mint, laced with a hint of eucalyptus, creates a fragrance that is down-to-earth and cleansing, perfect for the naturalist in all of us. NOTES: Rosemary, mint, eucalyptus.

Spearmint & Cinnamon

A combination of two essential oils, this enchanting fragrance reveals spearmint’s youthful notes grounded by subtle tones of cinnamon and ginger, invigorating the senses. It is both cool and spicy at once. NOTES: Spearmint, cinnamon, ginger.

Thai Basil & Sage

A tropical herb used among Asian cultures for centuries, Thai basil was planted among the gardens of sacred temples for both its spiritual and ritualistic purposes. Its distinctive fragrance combined with sage creates a grounded, earthy bouquet NOTES: Thai basil, sage, anise, bark.

Wildflower

Throughout the high alpine valleys of the Rocky Mountains, an extraordinary event happens each summer. Wildflower season is a special time of year when mother nature throws open her doors and reveals all that has been hidden by winter: fields of wildflowers in dozens of varieties, cool mountain streams and shady alcoves covered in delicate moss. NOTES: Bluebell, lily of the valley, rose, leafy greens, melon.
