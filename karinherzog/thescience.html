Why is Oxygen So Important For Our Skin?

Oxygen is found naturally in the skin and is essential to hydrate, de- toxify and nourish the skin cells. Its organic properties provide cells with the water and energy they need to produce collagen fibers and elastin, which help to repair the skin and fight the signs of aging.

Oxygen is also one of nature’s strongest disinfectants and helps to kill bacteria in, and on the skin.

As we get older, oxygen levels in the skin decline. By the age of just 25, there is already a substantial decline (up to 40%) in the skin’s oxygen level compared to that of a teenager. This depletion is accelerated by environmental and lifestyle factors such as smoking pollution, drinking alcohol, stress or illness.

As a result the skin begins to look dull, blemished and lifeless. It does not function sufficiently on its own, and is more prone to the effects of aging.
